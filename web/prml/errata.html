2.160

$$ p(x \mid \mu, a, b) = \int_0^\infty N(x \mid \mu, \tau^{-1}) \mathrm{Gam}(\tau \mid a, b) d\tau $$ ガンマ分布の定義から $$ \mathrm{Gam}(\tau \mid a, b) = \frac{1}{\Gamma(a)}b^a \tau^{a-1} \exp(-b\tau) $$ $\nu = 2a, \lambda = a/b, and \eta = \tau b/a$ とすると $$ a = \frac{\nu}{2} \\ b = \frac{a}{\lambda} = \frac{\nu}{2 \lambda} \\ \tau = \eta \frac{a}{b} = \eta \lambda $$ $a, b, \tau$に代入して $$ = \frac{1}{\Gamma(\frac{\nu}{2})} (\frac{\nu}{2 \lambda})^\frac{\nu}{2} (\eta \lambda)^{\frac{\nu}{2}-1} \exp(- \frac{\nu}{2 \lambda} \eta \lambda) \\ = \frac{1}{\Gamma(\frac{\nu}{2})} (\frac{\nu \eta}{2})^\frac{\nu}{2} (\eta \lambda)^{-1} \exp(- \frac{\nu}{2} \eta) \\ = \frac{1}{\Gamma(\frac{\nu}{2})} (\frac{\nu}{2})^{\frac{\nu}{2} - 1} \frac{\nu}{2} \eta^\frac{\nu}{2} (\eta \lambda)^{-1} \exp(- \frac{\nu}{2} \eta) \\ = \frac{1}{\Gamma(\frac{\nu}{2})} (\frac{\nu}{2})^{\frac{\nu}{2} - 1} \eta^\frac{\nu}{2} \exp(- \frac{\nu}{2} \eta) \cdot \frac{\nu}{2 \eta \lambda} \\ \mathrm{Gam}(\eta \mid \frac{\nu}{2}, \frac{\nu}{2}) \frac{\nu}{2 \eta \lambda} $$
